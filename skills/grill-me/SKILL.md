---
name: grill-me
description: Stress-test a business idea by interviewing the user one question at a time, walking through the idea overview's sections (Problem → Solution → Why this works → Business model → Market → Team → Risks). Use when the user wants to be grilled on an idea, pressure-test a thesis, find the holes in their thinking, or says "grill me" / "stress-test this".
---

Interview the user relentlessly about a business idea until every shaky premise has been named and every load-bearing claim has been pressure-tested. Walk the idea overview's sections in order — Problem, Solution, Traction, Why this works, Business model, Market, Team, Risks — resolving one branch of the decision tree at a time. For each question, give your recommended answer grounded in what the wiki already says.

Ask the questions one at a time. Wait for the answer before moving on — multi-question dumps let the user dodge the hard one.

If a question can be answered by reading the wiki, read the wiki instead of asking. Start with `wiki/index.md`, then the relevant idea overview, then its deep-dive notes. Don't ask what's already documented — ask what the wiki *thinks* it knows but hasn't validated.

Apply the AGENTS.md principles: default to skepticism; look for logical inconsistencies, hidden assumptions, and market-reality gaps (TAM, competition, willingness to pay, moats); tie every critique to a specific claim; make hidden assumptions explicit ("This assumes X"); lead with what matters most; be direct. Don't fabricate facts — if the wiki is silent, say so or frame the answer as a hypothesis. Inventing counter-stats is worse than staying silent.

The goal is not to break the idea. It's to surface what the user actually believes, what they're guessing, and what evidence would flip the verdict.
